Aim 2: Predict Energy from micronutrients only (macros & fatty-acid detail excluded)

Interpretation: Models use vitamins, minerals, carotenoids, choline, etc., but not
protein, fat, carbohydrate, sugars, water, fiber, alcohol, or individual SFA/MUFA/PUFA
chains — so R² is expected to be higher compare to the models that have access to macronutrient information.

Linear models perform poorly on this caloric regression task. 
This proves the non-linear relationship between micronutrients and food caloric content. 
In this test, Random Forest Regression achieves best predictive performance.

Data: food_nutrient_conc.csv 
Response: Energy  | Micronutrient predictors: 34 
Excluded (examples): protein, fat, carbs, sugars, water, fiber, alcohol, cholesterol,
  total SFA/MUFA/PUFA, and all MUFA/PUFA/SFA detail columns.
